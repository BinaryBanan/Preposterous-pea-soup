Finnish Pea Soup: A Culinary Disaster

Finnish pea soup (hernekeitto) is a thick, green soup made of dried peas, pork, and mustard. Traditionally eaten on Thursdays, it has a long history—but does that make it good? Absolutely not.

Why Finnish Pea Soup is Awful

  • The Texture: It’s a mushy, thick, and unappealing sludge.
  • The Smell: A combination of boiled peas and overcooked pork is not pleasant.
  • The Color: Greenish-yellow soup that looks like it was made in a chemistry lab.
  • The Taste: Bland and oddly sweet, unless drowned in mustard.
  • It’s Always Served with Pancakes: As if they knew they needed something better to distract you.

While some Finns enjoy this soup, many outsiders (and even locals) wonder why it still exists. Perhaps it’s tradition—or maybe they just have no choice.
